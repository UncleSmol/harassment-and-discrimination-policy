Harassment and Discrimination Policy

Purpose

Villa Roma Hotel is committed to fostering a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for all employees, trainees, and guests. This policy establishes guidelines to prevent, address, and resolve any form of harassment or discrimination to ensure a safe and supportive workplace.

Scope

This policy applies to all Villa Roma Hotel employees, trainees, contractors, vendors, and guests. It covers conduct in the workplace, training areas, and any Villa Roma Hotel-sponsored events or activities, including those that occur off-site or online.

Definitions

Harassment

Any unwanted behavior that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment. This includes, but is not limited to, verbal, physical, or visual conduct that demeans, threatens, or humiliates.

  • Examples: Derogatory remarks, offensive jokes, unwanted physical contact, stalking, or any behavior that could reasonably cause distress.

Sexual Harassment

A form of harassment involving unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other conduct of a sexual nature that affects employment, creates an intimidating environment, or interferes with work performance.

  • Examples: Inappropriate touching, suggestive comments, sharing sexually explicit material, or any action that could be seen as sexual intimidation.

Discrimination

Unfair treatment of individuals based on characteristics such as race, gender, religion, age, disability, nationality, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

  • Examples: Denying promotion opportunities, assigning tasks unfairly, or any conduct that treats employees unequally due to a protected characteristic.

Policy Guidelines

Zero Tolerance Policy

Villa Roma Hotel has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment and discrimination. All incidents are taken seriously and will be promptly addressed. Retaliation against individuals who report such behaviors in good faith is also strictly prohibited.

Expectations for Professional Conduct

  • Respectful Behavior: All employees and trainees are expected to maintain professionalism and respect toward colleagues, guests, and visitors.
  • Inclusivity: Everyone is encouraged to promote an inclusive environment, where diversity is valued and appreciated.
  • Reporting Responsibility: Employees, trainees, and managers are encouraged to report any incidents of harassment or discrimination they experience or witness.

Reporting Procedures

  • Confidential Reporting: Employees and trainees can report incidents confidentially to HR, a manager, or a designated representative without fear of retaliation.
  • How to Report: Reports can be made in person, by phone, or through a secure email. To assist in investigating, provide as much detail as possible, including names, dates, and a description of the incident.
  • Anonymous Reporting: If preferred, reports may be submitted anonymously through the hotel's anonymous reporting system. While anonymous reports are taken seriously, full anonymity may limit the extent of the investigation.

Investigation Process

  • Prompt Response: Upon receiving a report, HR will initiate a confidential investigation within 48 hours, following fair and unbiased practices.
  • Interviews and Evidence Gathering: HR will conduct interviews with the involved parties and any witnesses, gather relevant documentation, and assess all evidence impartially.
  • Resolution Timeline: Investigations will be resolved within 14 days of the initial report. If additional time is required, HR will communicate any delays to the involved parties.

Corrective Actions

  • Disciplinary Measures: If harassment or discrimination is confirmed, corrective actions may include verbal or written warnings, mandatory training, suspension, termination, or any action appropriate to the severity of the offense.
  • Follow-Up and Support: HR will conduct follow-ups to ensure the resolution is effective and that no further issues have arisen. Support may include counseling or external resources for affected employees or trainees.

Protection Against Retaliation

Villa Roma Hotel strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee or trainee who, in good faith, reports harassment, discrimination, or cooperates in an investigation. Retaliation may lead to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Policy Review and Updates

This Harassment and Discrimination Policy will be reviewed annually to stay current with laws and best practices. Any changes will be communicated to all employees and trainees to ensure continued awareness and compliance.

Acknowledgment

All employees and trainees are required to review, understand, and sign this policy upon joining Villa Roma Hotel. By signing, they agree to comply with all guidelines and uphold the hotel's commitment to a safe and respectful work environment.
